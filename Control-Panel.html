What is a Clap preference?

You may have noticed a Clap option in your device panel. This is your clap preference.

If a clap preference is enabled for a device, your Device Interfacer's microphone will listen for a clap. If you clap, your device's power state will be toggled!

You can only set a clap preference for one device at a time. The clap preference functionality works best in environments with low levels of noise, to prevent interference or accidental toggles.

See the diagrams on the right for a pictoral explanation. Try it out for yourself!

The Radio:bit does not have a microphone. Your clap must be heard by the Device Interfacer.

What is a Device Interfacer/Radio:bit?
Plusmore's flagship duo! The Device Interfacer manages connected devices according to your configurations on this page. The Radio:bit acts as a wireless dongle which passes information onto the Device Interfacer. For more info, visit the Interfacer Suite's Product Page.

The WebUSB interface doesn't show up when I click connect!
To use WebUSB, ensure you are running the latest version of a chrome-based web browser

"BBC micro:bit CMSIS-DAP" isn't showing up!
There are a number of possiblities.

  • Your device has shown up under a different name, such as "LPC1768"
  • Your Micro-USB cable is only capable of transmitting power. It must be capable of transferring data.
  • Your Micro-USB cable is faulty, or your Micro:bit's USB port is damaged. Replacements will be needed.

My device isn't responding to claps!
Ensure the following conditions are met.

  • Your environment is quiet enough for claps to be detected
  • You are using a Micro:bit 2.0 as the Device Interfacer
  • Your device is connected to the correct pin.
    (LEDs should be connected to Pin 0)
